Manual cowpea thresher for small scale farmers

The project aims to address the challenges of traditional cowpea threshing methods, which are labor-intensive, time-consuming, and lead to poor seed quality. By providing an affordable, ergonomic, and sustainable solution, it seeks to enhance agricultural productivity, support the livelihoods of small-scale farmers, and promote environmentally friendly practices in Burkina Faso.

 

The manual cowpea thresher is designed as a cost-effective and sustainable machine for small scale farmers. The thresher utilizes a drum-and-beater mechanism to separate cowpea seeds from pods, significantly reducing labor and improving seed quality. Built with locally available materials like E335 steel, and operates without electricity, ensuring accessibility or rural farmers.
